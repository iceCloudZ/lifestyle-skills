# NVC Style

Use this skill to help a person express conflict through observations, feelings, needs, and requests.

## Use When

- The person wants to say something difficult without escalating.
- The conflict is tangled with blame or assumptions.
- A concrete request would help.

## Avoid When

- There is safety risk or coercion.
- The other party is acting in bad faith.
- Legal or formal intervention is needed.

## Evidence Card

evidence_level: C
evidence_type: communication framework and conflict de-escalation practice
why_included: This lens helps users turn blame or interpretation into observations, feelings, needs, and concrete requests.
known_limits: Evidence is limited and the method can sound mechanical or manipulative if used without genuine care.
safety_boundaries: Do not use empathy language to pressure unsafe contact, excuse harm, or negotiate with coercion.

## Reasoning Flow

### 1. Pre-check: Should this lens be used here?

Use when the user needs a clearer, less blaming message and the relationship context is safe enough for direct communication. Avoid if safety or bad faith is present.

### 2. Key assessment questions

- What observable fact happened, without interpretation?
- What feeling and need belong to the user?
- What concrete request would be doable and bounded?

### 3. Reasoning branches

- Blame-heavy draft: convert judgment into observation and feeling.
- Vague frustration: identify the underlying need before making a request.
- Boundary need: make the request specific and time-bounded.
- Unsafe contact: do not script vulnerable disclosure; hand off or suggest safety planning.

### 4. Minimum complete answer

- Separate observation from interpretation.
- Name a feeling without blame.
- Name the need or value.
- Make one concrete, doable request.

### 5. Hand-off conditions

Hand off for coercion, threats, abuse risk, legal conflict, HR/formal disputes, or any situation where direct contact may increase harm.

## Thinking Steps

1. Separate observation from interpretation.
2. Name feelings without blame.
3. Identify the underlying need.
4. Form one concrete, doable request.
5. Keep the message short and direct.

## Output Format

- Observation: fact-based statement.
- Feeling: internal state.
- Need: what matters.
- Request: specific ask.

## Safety

Do not use empathy language to excuse harm or pressure someone into unsafe contact.
