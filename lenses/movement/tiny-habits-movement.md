# Tiny Habits Movement

Apply this lens directly. Do not offer lens choices or route back to the entry skill. The user has selected this thinking framework.

## Purpose

Convert movement goals into tiny, easy behaviors attached to existing routines.

## Use When

- The person has failed several plans.
- Time, motivation, or confidence is low.
- The first goal is consistency, not performance.

## Avoid When

- The person needs race prep or advanced training.
- The person already has reliable exercise habits.
- The problem is medical restriction rather than habit design.

## Evidence Card

evidence_level: B
evidence_type: behavior-design framework and habit coaching practice

known_limits: It is not a full training plan and may be too small for users who already have stable exercise habits.

not_for: advanced programming, race prep, medical restriction, acute pain, or users needing performance periodization

## Reasoning Flow

### 1. Pre-check: Should this lens be used here?

Use when the user repeatedly fails plans, feels low motivation, or needs a habit anchor. If the issue is injury, medical restriction, or performance programming, use another path.

### 2. Key assessment questions

- What daily routine already happens reliably?
- What movement could be made almost too easy?
- What friction causes the current plan to fail?

### 3. Reasoning branches

- No time: shrink to one minute or less.
- Low confidence: choose a movement that feels impossible to fail.
- Forgetting: attach the movement to a strong anchor.
- Shame cycle: emphasize celebration and identity, not discipline.

### 4. Minimum complete answer

- Name the anchor.
- Define a tiny movement after the anchor.
- Include a positive closure or celebration.
- Avoid turning it into a full workout.

### 5. Hand-off conditions

Hand off for acute pain, medical restrictions, advanced training goals, or cases where mental health support is more relevant than habit design.

## Thinking Steps

1. Identify an existing daily anchor.
2. Shrink the movement until it feels almost too easy.
3. Attach the behavior to the anchor.
4. Add a small celebration or positive closure.
5. Plan for expansion only after consistency appears.

## Output Format

- Fit: why this lens applies.
- Tiny habit: after I do X, I will do Y.
- Friction cut: what to make easier.
- Next step: try once today.

## Safety

Do not frame inconsistency as laziness. Keep movements pain-free.
