# Strength Baseline

Apply this lens directly. Do not offer lens choices or route back to the entry skill. The user has selected this thinking framework.

## Use When

- The person wants to get stronger.
- The person is new, returning, or aging-focused.
- The goal is baseline resilience rather than peak performance.

## Avoid When

- There is acute pain or unresolved injury.
- Technique support is unavailable for risky lifts.
- The person needs an advanced training cycle.

## Evidence Card

evidence_level: A
evidence_type: physical activity guidelines and strength-training practice

## Reasoning Flow

### 1. Pre-check: Should this lens be used here?

Check these before continuing. This step is required, not optional:

- Ask: "Any current pain, recent injury, or medical restriction on exercise?"
- If yes: hand off to a physiotherapist or doctor before programming. Do not give a session.
- If no: proceed to step 2.

### 2. Classify the branch

Pick exactly one:

- **Complete beginner, no equipment**: Give 3-4 bodyweight movements (squat, push-up variation, hinge/bridge, carry or plank). Sets: 2. Reps: 8-12 or "as many as feel easy." Frequency: 2 days per week.
- **Has dumbbells or basic equipment**: Add load to the same patterns. Start light enough that 12 reps feel easy. Progress when 12 reps at current weight feel comfortable for 2 sessions in a row.
- **Returning after a break**: Start at 60% of previous volume. Rebuild for 2 weeks before adding load.
- **Very limited time**: Give a single-movement session. 3 sets of one compound movement (squat or push variation). Total time: under 10 minutes.

### 3. Deliver the minimum complete answer

Output all four parts directly. Do not offer a menu of options—give ONE concrete session.

1. **Fit**: One sentence on why this is a baseline strength issue.
2. **Starter session**: A specific session with movements, sets, reps, and frequency. Example:
   ```
   Session A (2x per week):
   - Bodyweight squat: 2 sets of 10
   - Incline push-up (hands on chair): 2 sets of 8
   - Glute bridge: 2 sets of 12
   ```
3. **Safety note**: One specific warning tied to the user's context. Always include: "Stop if you feel sharp or joint pain. Mild muscle soreness is normal; pain during the movement is not."
4. **Progression rule**: One sentence on how to progress. Example: "When you can do 12 reps of every movement for two sessions in a row, add 2 reps or a small amount of weight."

### 4. Hand-off conditions

Hand off for acute pain, neurological symptoms, injury rehab, medical restrictions, or technique-dependent heavy lifting.

## Safety

Do not push through pain. Recommend qualified coaching for uncertain technique. Always check for pain or injury before giving a session.
