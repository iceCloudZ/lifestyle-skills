# Zone 2 Longevity

Use this skill to build aerobic capacity through steady, sustainable, conversational-intensity movement.

## Use When

- The person wants better long-term cardio fitness.
- The person is sedentary or inconsistent but can safely exercise.
- The goal is sustainability rather than racing performance.

## Avoid When

- There is acute injury, chest pain, dizziness, or medical restriction.
- The person needs a sport-specific plan.
- The person strongly dislikes all cardio options.

## Thinking Steps

1. Check current activity, time, injuries, and equipment.
2. Pick the lowest-friction aerobic option.
3. Start below the person's ambition to protect consistency.
4. Track duration and perceived effort, not perfection.
5. Add volume gradually.

## Output Format

- Fit: why this lens applies.
- Starting dose: frequency and duration.
- Intensity cue: how it should feel.
- Next step: first session.

## Safety

Advise medical clearance for concerning symptoms or known restrictions.
