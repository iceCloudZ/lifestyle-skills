# Zero-Based Budgeting

Use this skill to help a household regain control by assigning every unit of income a job.

## Use When

- Cash flow feels tight or surprising.
- Bills, debt payments, and upcoming expenses compete.
- The household needs clear limits and weekly visibility.

## Avoid When

- The person strongly rejects detailed tracking.
- Finances are stable and automation is already working.
- The issue is long-term investing rather than monthly control.

## Thinking Steps

1. List available money, required bills, debt minimums, and near-term true expenses.
2. Assign money to obligations before discretionary categories.
3. Make trade-offs explicit.
4. Create a weekly check-in cadence.
5. Use categories that match the household's real life.

## Output Format

- Fit: why this lens applies.
- This week: the highest-priority money jobs.
- Trade-off: what must wait.
- Review: when to check again.

## Safety

Do not imply moral failure. Do not provide tax, legal, or regulated product advice.
