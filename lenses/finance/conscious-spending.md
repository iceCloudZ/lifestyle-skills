# Conscious Spending

Use this skill to help a household align spending with values, joy, obligations, and future goals.

## Use When

- The person has enough basic stability but feels unclear where money goes.
- The question involves balancing enjoyment, savings, fixed costs, and guilt.
- Strict budgeting has failed or feels too punitive.

## Avoid When

- There is a debt crisis or no emergency buffer.
- The person asks which security, fund, insurance, or loan product to buy.
- The situation needs licensed financial advice.

## Reasoning Flow

### 1. Pre-check: Should this lens be used here?

Use when the household has enough stability to make choices and the problem is guilt, unclear priorities, or spending that does not match values. If survival expenses or debt crisis dominate, switch to cash-flow triage or professional support.

### 2. Key assessment questions

- Which spending category creates guilt or conflict?
- What joy or value should be protected if possible?
- Which fixed cost, subscription, or habit is most adjustable?

### 3. Reasoning branches

- Shame spiral: separate values from self-judgment and choose one neutral review step.
- Joy conflict: protect one intentional joy category while trimming lower-value leakage.
- Fixed-cost pressure: review recurring costs before cutting small pleasures.
- Couple disagreement: make categories shared, individual, and future-oriented before debating amounts.

### 4. Minimum complete answer

- Name the value or tension behind the spending.
- Protect at least one intentional category when safe.
- Identify one trade-off or review point.
- Give one concrete action this week.

### 5. Hand-off conditions

Hand off for debt default, legal/tax questions, product recommendations, or financial abuse. Keep the lens focused on values and household trade-offs.

## Thinking Steps

1. Identify fixed costs, savings goals, guilt-free spending, and value categories.
2. Separate real constraints from shame-based reactions.
3. Look for one or two high-leverage adjustments, not total austerity.
4. Preserve at least one joyful spending category if possible.
5. Give a small next review step.

## Output Format

- Fit: why this lens applies.
- Trade-off: what to protect and what to adjust.
- Next step: one concrete action this week.
- Boundary: note when a professional advisor is needed.

## Safety

Do not recommend specific investments, loans, insurance products, or tax actions.
