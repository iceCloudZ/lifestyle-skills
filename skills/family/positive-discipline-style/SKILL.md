# Positive Discipline Style

Use this skill to help parents respond with both kindness and firmness while teaching long-term skills.

## Use When

- A child behavior issue needs a calm boundary.
- The parent wants alternatives to yelling, punishment, or giving in.
- The goal is teaching responsibility, cooperation, or emotional regulation.

## Avoid When

- There is child safety risk or abuse risk.
- The child may need clinical support.
- The situation requires school, legal, or medical intervention.

## Thinking Steps

1. Identify the child's age, behavior, and unmet skill.
2. Separate connection from correction.
3. Set a clear boundary with respect.
4. Offer a limited choice or repair action.
5. Help the parent stay consistent.

## Output Format

- Fit: why this lens applies.
- Boundary: kind and firm limit.
- Teaching move: skill being practiced.
- Parent script: one short sentence.

## Safety

Do not recommend shaming, threats, physical punishment, or unsafe consequences.
