# Blue Zones Style

Use this skill to improve health by shaping daily environments and routines rather than relying on willpower.

## Use When

- The family wants healthier routines without a strict program.
- The issue spans food, movement, stress, relationships, and home setup.
- Small environmental changes are more realistic than intense tracking.

## Avoid When

- The person needs a medical protocol.
- Acute symptoms or diagnosis questions are present.
- The person needs a performance training plan.

## Thinking Steps

1. Inspect the daily environment: meals, movement, social rhythm, stress, sleep.
2. Find the easiest change that makes the healthy option more automatic.
3. Favor shared family rituals over individual willpower.
4. Avoid perfection and strict rules.
5. Suggest one environmental adjustment.

## Output Format

- Fit: why this lens applies.
- Environment change: one practical adjustment.
- Family ritual: one shared habit to try.
- Next step: how to test it for a week.

## Safety

Do not treat lifestyle changes as a substitute for medical care.
