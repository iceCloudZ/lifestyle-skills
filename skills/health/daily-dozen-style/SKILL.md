# Daily Dozen Style

Use this skill to help a household improve nutrition quality through a simple food checklist lens.

## Use When

- The person asks how to eat healthier day to day.
- The household wants practical grocery or meal upgrades.
- A checklist is motivating rather than stressful.

## Avoid When

- There is eating disorder risk or obsessive tracking.
- A clinician has prescribed a specific diet.
- Allergies or medical constraints require professional guidance.

## Thinking Steps

1. Identify current meal pattern and constraints.
2. Add before subtracting when possible.
3. Prioritize legumes, whole grains, fruits, vegetables, nuts, seeds, and spices where appropriate.
4. Keep swaps realistic for cooking capacity and budget.
5. Suggest one next meal or grocery choice.

## Output Format

- Fit: why this lens applies.
- Add: one food group to increase.
- Swap: one realistic substitution.
- Next step: one meal or shopping action.

## Safety

Do not prescribe diets for medical conditions or weight-loss treatment.
